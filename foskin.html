FUNCTIONS OF SKIN

  1. Protective Function:-

    Skin forms the covering of all organs of the body and protects the organs from the following factors:

    1. Bacteria And Toxic Substances:

      Skin covers the organs of the body and protect the organs from having direct contact with external enviornment.Thus it prevents the bacterial infection.

    2. Mechanical Blow:

      Skin is not tightly placed over underlying organs or tissues.So, the mechanical impact of any blow to the skin is not transmitted to the underlying tissues.

    3. Ultraviolet Rays:

      Skin protects the body from ultraviolet rays of sun.

  2. Sensory Function:-

    • Largest sense organ in the body.
    • The nerve ending has many receptors named as specialized cutaneous receptor.
    • these receptors convey all type of sensation to brain via afferent nerves.

  3. Storage Function:-

    • Skin stores fat, water, chlorides, and sugars.
    • Also stores blood by the dilation of cutaneous blood vessels.

  4. Synthetic Function:-

    • Vitamin D₃ is synthesized in skin by the action of ultra violet rays from sunlight on cholestrol.

  5. Regulation Of Body Temprature:-

    • Skin plays very important role in the regulation of body temprature.
    • Excess heat is lost from the body through skin by radiation, conduction, convection and evaporation.
    • Sweat glands of the skin play an active par in heat loss, by secreting sweat.

  6. Regulation Of Water And Electrolyte Balance:-

    • Skin regulates water balance and electrolyte balance by excreting water and salts through sweat.

  7. Excretory Function:-

    • Skin excretes smal; quantities of waste materials like urea, salts & fatty substances.

  8. Absorptive Function:-

    • Skin absorbs Fat-soluble substances and some oinments.

  9. Secretory Function:-

    • Sweat » through sweat glands
    • Sebum » through sebaccous glands
    • Sebum keeps the skin smooth and moist.
